About Through The Kitchen Hole

Through The Kitchen Hole is a rock band based out of Eau Claire, WI. The band formed in early 2018 when a few friends decided to play in a school talent show. Since then, TTKH has grown both in size and talent. TTKH plays at many venues across the Chippewa Valley such as The Plus, Jake's Supper Club, and the Relay For Life. They also competed in the Rockonsin garage band competition at Summerfest in 2018, and the Bitter Jester Music Festival in 2019.


TTKH has a self titled album out on all major streaming platforms. The album features four of their favorite cover songs as well as one original song titled "Transitions", which was written in 2018. They are currently working on a new album of all original tracks.

Abby Berthiaume

Abby Berthiaume started singing in choirs in middle school and continued throughout high school. She took voice lessons for 5 years under different teachers with her main vocal coach being Luke Otto. She joined Through The Kitchen Hole in 2018 when they were looking for a full time singer. Abby is currently studying Early Childhood Special Education at the University of Wisconsin-Stout

Braedon Laundrie

Braedon began playing the drums at nine years old in Eau Claire, Wisconsin, where he took lessons under Ron Guard, and later under Jim Gallagher at age 17. He has played in jazz bands, jazz combos, rock bands, and orchestra pits throughout Wisconsin. Braedon officially joined Through The Kitchen Hole in March of 2019, and is currently studying at the University of Wisconsin-Eau Claire.

Katherine Seeger

Katherine Seeger is a classical and jazz violinist, bluegrass fiddler, and bassist from Eau Claire, Wisconsin. She first started studying violin with her father at the age of 4. She joined Through The Kitchen Hole in 2018. She is currently completing her Bachelor's in Music Violin Performance at the University of Minnesota as a student of Kyu-Young Kim. She has previously studied with Young-Nam Kim.

Toby Moszer

Toby Moszer started playing French horn when he was 11, and dropped that in favor of guitar when he was 14. He was originally self taught, and then took lessons under Paul Brandt. Toby played in Jazz I at Memorial High School during his junior and senior year. He is a founding member of the band. Toby is currently studying computer science at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities.

Zach Menter

Zachary Menter started playing guitar when he was 8 years old. Originally teaching himself and learning from his dad, he eventually took lessons for a few years under Lucas Fischer. Zach was in choir all through middle school and high school, and is a founding member of the band. He is currently studying computer science at the University of Wisconsin-Eau Claire.
